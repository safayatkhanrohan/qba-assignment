What Is Business Forecasting?

  • Definition: Systematic use of historical data, statistical models & expert judgment to predict future metrics.
  • Key Metrics: Sales, costs, cash-flow, headcount.

“Forecasting is your business’s weather report—so you never get caught in a storm.”

Methods Overview

  • Delphi & Expert Panels
  • Market Research & Focus Groups
  • Time-Series (MA, Exponential Smoothing)
  • Causal Models (Regression, ARIMAX)

When to use: New product/market → Qualitative; Established trends → Quantitative

Qualitative Methods

  • Delphi Technique: Round-robin surveys of 10–15 experts until consensus.

    Example: Gartner’s 2023 AI market forecast: US$62B by year-end (±10%).

  • Market Research: Surveys & focus groups of 500+ consumers.

    Example: Nike’s Spring ’24 footwear survey (N=1,200) drove 15% design tweaks.

+ Fast, flexible   – Subjective, bias risk

Quantitative Methods (Causal Model)

Linking US GDP to Amazon Revenue

Quarter US GDP (T) Amazon Rev (B)
Q1 2023 25.46 127.4
Q2 2023 25.86 134.4
Q3 2023 26.02 143.4

Regression: Rev = -546 + 26.4 × GDP

Forecast if GDP = 26.5 T: ≈ 154.0 B

Applications in Business

  • Supply Chain & Inventory: Walmart cuts stockouts by 33% across 120K SKUs.
  • Production Scheduling: Toyota reduces idle time by 12%.
  • Budgeting & Cost Control: Microsoft improves ROI by 18%.
  • Strategic Decisions: Tesla locks in battery lithium contracts early.

Importance & Impact

  • Risk Reduction: Avoid overproduction or stockouts.
  • Competitive Edge: Netflix forecasted 8.8M net adds vs. consensus 5M (Q1 2024).
  • Resource Optimization: Saved 7% in carrying costs on $50M capex.
  • Real-World Win: Amazon cut order delays by 40% in Q4 staffing.
